Data Centre Weekly Overview

Weekly Overview

June 30 – July 6, 2026 · North America focus
Week in review: Power dominated the week. Chevron's 20-year deal to supply Microsoft's West Texas AI campus on-site marks a structural shift — hyperscalers are pairing directly with generators to bypass grid queues, while Texas pilots centralized ERCOT rules for large behind-the-meter loads and NERC warns AI load growth threatens grid reliability. Governments are responding on the revenue side too: Virginia's first-ever data centre power tax took effect July 1. In Canada, OpenAI is evaluating Stargate capacity north of the border while Hamilton's data centre NDA controversy makes transparency a live project risk. Note: only one daily report (July 6) was available this week, so this overview draws on limited coverage.
1

Chevron lands 20-year Microsoft deal to power West Texas AI campus

EnergyConstruction

Chevron will supply dedicated on-site power for a planned data centre campus near Pecos, Texas — one of the largest pairings of compute infrastructure and on-site generation in the US, delivering roughly 250 MW of utility power by early 2028 with expected capacity of 2 GW at full delivery.

Why it matters: An oil major becoming a hyperscaler's power provider signals a structural shift — generation is moving on-site to bypass grid queues entirely.

OpenAI considers Stargate data centre capacity in Canada

CanadaConstruction

OpenAI is evaluating Canada as a location for Stargate data centre capacity, potentially bringing part of its massive AI infrastructure buildout north of the border.

Why it matters: A Stargate site would be transformative for the Canadian market — power availability, cool climate, and sovereign-AI policy momentum make Canada a serious contender.

Meta plans cloud business to sell excess AI compute

Meta is developing plans for a cloud infrastructure business, selling access to both AI compute capacity and its models — a strategic pivot to monetize its enormous data centre buildout.

4

Texas tests new rules for AI campuses behind existing power plants

EnergyPolicy

Texas is overhauling how large loads of 75 MW or more connect to the grid, replacing a utility-by-utility approach with centralized ERCOT evaluation — a shift prompted by an avalanche of data centre and AI-related power requests.

Why it matters: ERCOT's framework for behind-the-meter AI campuses will likely become the template other grid operators copy — or avoid.

Microsoft's $3.3B Wisconsin AI campus moves from construction to operations

ConstructionTech

Microsoft cut the ribbon on its $3.3 billion Mount Pleasant facility — the first data centre at the campus and one of the company's highest-profile AI infrastructure projects, now fully online.

Why it matters: One of the largest AI-purpose-built campuses in North America is now live — a benchmark for the scale and speed of hyperscaler AI buildouts.

Amazon's $10B Missouri data centre underscores hyperscalers' search for power

ConstructionEnergy

Amazon is investing $10 billion in a new Missouri data centre campus while Google commits $15 billion to a separate project — $25 billion of hyperscale development landing in a single county as operators chase available power.

Why it matters: Missouri's emergence as a hub shows the market moving to wherever power exists — secondary markets are the new frontier.

7

NERC flags AI data centre grid risks in reliability report

EnergyPolicy

North America's grid reliability watchdog warns that AI data centre load growth poses material risks to bulk power system reliability, citing volatile load behaviour and interconnection speed pressures.

Why it matters: NERC findings shape utility planning and interconnection rules continent-wide — including Canadian provinces under NERC standards.

8

Virginia's first-ever data centre power tax takes effect

PolicyEnergy

Virginia's new consumption tax of $0.011/kWh on all electricity used by data centres began July 1, projected to raise ~$600 million annually. At least one operator is challenging related regulatory requirements in court.

Why it matters: The world's largest data centre market just put a price on consumption — expect other jurisdictions, including Canadian provinces, to study it closely.

9

Hamilton-owned corporation signs NDA over proposed AI data centre

CanadaPolicy

A City of Hamilton-owned energy corporation signed an NDA with Slate Asset Management over a proposed AI data centre on former Stelco lands. Council rejected Slate's land-division proposal after community pushback; Slate is appealing. Microsoft, notably, announced it will no longer sign NDAs with local governments.

Why it matters: Transparency is becoming a competitive differentiator in Canadian site development — secrecy is now a project risk.

10

Equinix turns data centre waste heat into Milan city heating

TechEnergy

Equinix and A2A are capturing server waste heat and feeding it into Milan's district heating network — set to become one of Europe's largest heat-export projects outside the Nordics. (Global story with North American relevance.)

Why it matters: Heat reuse at this scale is a proof point for North American operators — especially in Canada, where district heating and cold climates make the economics compelling.
