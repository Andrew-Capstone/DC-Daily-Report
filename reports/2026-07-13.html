1

QuiX Quantum unveils photonic Dedalo architecture built for HPC data centres

Tech

QuiX Quantum introduced its Dedalo architecture, which it calls the first logical-qubit-based photonic quantum computer built for integration into traditional HPC data centres, using CMOS-compatible silicon-nitride photonic chips that run at room temperature. The design uses measurement-based quantum computing with error-correcting logical qubits to overcome photon loss — the central barrier to scaling photonic systems — and is compatible with standard fiber-optic telecom gear already inside data centres.

Why it matters: A room-temperature quantum architecture designed to slot into existing HPC facilities, rather than requiring dedicated cryogenic infrastructure, could be the first practical bridge between quantum and classical AI data centre computing.

2

ECL and PowerCell strike hydrogen fuel cell deal to power US AI data centres at scale

EnergyTech

Data centre developer ECL and Sweden's PowerCell Group announced July 13 a firm purchase order for PowerCell's PS190 hydrogen fuel cell systems plus a non-binding MOU covering up to 300MW of additional capacity, backed by Bosch, PowerCell's largest shareholder. First deployment lands at ECL's 35MW CSC-1 campus in Santa Clara, building on an existing unit at its Mountain View facility, ahead of a planned 1GW hydrogen-powered Texas campus with Lambda as tenant.

Why it matters: PowerCell's CEO said the deal signals hydrogen-powered AI data centres "moving from first-of-kind toward industrial scale," giving hyperscalers a water-free, grid-independent power option as interconnection queues stay backed up.

3

EQT buys Copia Power from Carlyle, picking up a 9GW data centre power pipeline

EnergyFinance

Infrastructure investor EQT announced July 10 it has acquired Copia Power — an energy-and-data-centre developer that co-locates power generation, high-voltage transmission and data centre infrastructure at a single interconnection point — from Carlyle's infrastructure group. Copia currently operates or is building more than 2.6GW of energy assets and is developing over 9GW of grid-connected data centre capacity backed by 25GW of solar-plus-storage and 7GW of gas generation; deal terms weren't disclosed.

Why it matters: The deal shows infrastructure funds racing to buy entire "power-plus-land" platforms outright rather than just financing individual projects, as behind-the-meter generation becomes the scarcest asset in the AI buildout.

Siemens and FuelCell Energy team up on 100MW-plus fuel cell systems for data centres

Energy

Siemens and FuelCell Energy signed a non-binding memorandum of understanding July 10 to jointly develop distributed power systems — combining fuel cells, battery storage and microgrid controls — for data centres and other large industrial power users, with Siemens supplying electrical infrastructure for installations above 100MW. The partnership is separate from FuelCell Energy's binding deal last month to supply up to 380MW of fuel cell generation to Fit Energy USA for data centre deployment.

Why it matters: A major industrial electrical player joining forces with a fuel cell manufacturer signals distributed, non-combustion power generation is moving from boutique pilot projects toward standardized systems for hyperscale sites.

5

Meta's Alberta data centre will go live years before its dedicated gas plant is ready

CanadaEnergy

Meta's Sturgeon County data centre is expected to start operating within two to three years, but the adjacent $4.6-billion Greenlight Electricity Centre gas plant meant to power it won't produce electricity until the second half of 2030, BNN Bloomberg reported July 10. Meta holds rights to connect to Alberta's grid in the interim and has separately signed a 250MW supply deal with Capital Power for power starting in the second half of 2028.

Why it matters: The gap shows that even hyperscalers building dedicated, purpose-built power plants can't always sync generation and compute timelines, leaving campuses to lean on the public grid or interim contracts during construction.

6

Meta says its Alberta data centre will need almost no water for cooling

CanadaTech

Meta will use a closed-loop liquid cooling system at its Sturgeon County campus instead of evaporative cooling, meaning no water is consumed for routine cooling and on-site water use is limited to domestic needs, fire protection and equipment maintenance, the company's VP of data centre strategy told BNN Bloomberg July 9. Coolant absorbs heat directly from chips and is cooled by outside air — a technique well suited to Alberta's cold climate.

Why it matters: A near-zero-water design for one of Canada's largest data centres could become the template other cold-climate provinces point to as they weigh new hyperscale projects against local water concerns.

Alberta Premier Danielle Smith defends Meta data centre amid public anxiety

CanadaPolicy

Smith spent much of a July 11 call-in radio show reassuring Albertans about the Meta project, saying the province tells data centre developers "you can come, but you ought to build your own power and, better yet, build more than you need so you can sell back into the grid," and that Alberta has decommissioning and electronic-waste recycling plans in place should Meta ever shut the facility down.

Why it matters: With polling showing most Canadians would oppose a large AI data centre near their home, a premier publicly defending Alberta's flagship project shows how much political capital is riding on the buildout going smoothly.

Mara buys 1,200-acre Texas site for up to a 2GW data centre campus

FinanceConstruction

Bitcoin miner and AI infrastructure developer Mara announced July 13 it has agreed to acquire a powered site of more than 1,200 acres in Matagorda County, Texas, roughly 90 miles from Houston, from e-fuel company HIF USA, targeting up to 1GW of capacity by October 2027 and up to 2GW by April 2028. Mara is developing the site with partner Starwood Digital Ventures under a partnership formed in February.

Why it matters: The deal is another sign crypto miners are converting stranded power sites into large-scale AI compute campuses, treating access to reliable power as the primary asset rather than the land itself.

Cracks appear in the AI bond boom as SpaceX and Meta debt widens

Finance

SpaceX's new 6.65% 30-year bonds have traded above T+200 this week after pricing at T+175, and Meta's 6.30% 2056 bonds widened to T+145 versus levels as tight as T+120 a month ago, signalling cooling investor appetite even as AI-linked investment-grade issuance topped $218 billion through July 8 — already blowing past 2025's full-year total of $80.5 billion, the Motley Fool reported July 10.

Why it matters: After a year of AI bond deals being oversubscribed multiple times over, early signs of secondary-market indigestion suggest credit markets may be nearing the limits of their appetite for data centre debt.

10

Illinois lawmakers weigh mandating water-efficient cooling at all new data centres

PolicyEnergy

Illinois' stalled POWER Act would require new data centre cooling systems to be at least as water-efficient as closed-loop designs, an approach state Sen. Rachel Ventura says would bring "consistency, transparency and fairness" as individual towns write their own rules, Shaw Local reported July 10. Nearby projects vary widely: Yorkville's Project Cardinal uses about 15.5 million gallons of water a year, while Meta's DeKalb facility is capped at 200,000 gallons a day.

Why it matters: A statewide cooling standard would pre-empt the patchwork of local water rules Illinois towns have been writing project-by-project, potentially becoming a model other power- and water-constrained states copy.

11

Marietta, Georgia tables data centre rezoning, passes moratorium through year-end

Policy

Marietta's city council voted July 8 to table a contested rezoning request for an 18MW data centre on Powers Ferry Place after hours of public protest, with Councilwoman Cheryl Richardson requesting a sound-and-light study and a larger buffer space, and separately passed a temporary moratorium on all new data centres through Dec. 31 to evaluate community impacts.

Why it matters: Even a single contested project drew enough backlash to trigger a citywide pause, underscoring how quickly local opposition can escalate from one rezoning fight into a blanket moratorium.

12

Shoshone-Bannock Tribes formally oppose $2.6B AI data centre planned in Pocatello, Idaho

Policy

The Shoshone-Bannock Tribes of the Fort Hall Reservation have filed opposition to Lex Developments' proposed $2.6 billion, seven-building AI data centre on the former Hoku Materials site in Pocatello, citing risks to electricity rates, water supply and ceded tribal lands, Data Center Dynamics reported July 13, ahead of a city council appeal hearing scheduled for July 16. The project was initially denied for insufficient application detail in May and would draw up to 100MW of power by 2031.

Why it matters: Tribal opposition adds a legal and moral dimension largely absent from the wave of municipal moratoriums so far, and could shape how developers engage Indigenous stakeholders on future US site selection.
