1

National Grid Ventures invests $1.75B in Joulent to build gas plant powering 2 GW Microsoft data centre in Texas

EnergyFinance

National Grid's commercial arm has agreed to invest $1.75 billion for a 35% stake in Joulent, a US power infrastructure developer and Engine No. 1 portfolio company, to develop large-load power solutions — headlined by construction of a natural gas plant powering a 2 GW Microsoft data centre in Texas (July 3).

Why it matters: A major grid operator is now buying into behind-the-meter generation for hyperscalers — the clearest sign yet that dedicated on-site gas is becoming core utility strategy, not a workaround.

EnergyTechCanada

Toronto-headquartered Brookfield is expanding its partnership with Bloom Energy, growing its investment commitment to $25 billion to support fuel cell deployment across AI data centres (July 3). Fuel cells offer fast-to-deploy, on-site power that sidesteps grid interconnection queues.

Wacker launches silicon-based immersion cooling fluid as PFAS alternative

Tech

German chemicals firm Wacker has launched a data centre immersion cooling fluid, pitching its silicon-based product as a less harmful alternative to PFAS-based offerings (July 3). Immersion cooling is gaining traction as AI rack densities push past the limits of air cooling.

Why it matters: With PFAS "forever chemical" regulation tightening in the US and Canada, a viable silicon-based fluid removes a key regulatory risk from immersion cooling adoption in North America.

NTT Facilities develops modular construction system that could cut hyperscale build times by 50%

TechConstruction

NTT Facilities has developed a "Hyper Ready Module" modular construction system for hyperscale data centres that the company claims could cut construction timelines by 50 percent (July 3).

Why it matters: Speed-to-power is the industry's binding constraint — halving build time would be as valuable as finding new grid capacity, and modular methods transfer directly to North American projects.

CanadaConstruction

7

Hamilton-owned corporation signs NDA over proposed AI data centre as Slate appeals council rejection

CanadaPolicy

A City of Hamilton-owned energy corporation signed an NDA with Slate Asset Management over a proposed AI data centre on former Stelco steelmaking lands (July 2). Council rejected Slate's land-division proposal after hundreds turned out in opposition; Slate is appealing. Microsoft, by contrast, says it will no longer sign NDAs with local governments.

Why it matters: Transparency is becoming a competitive differentiator in Canadian site development — secrecy is now a project risk that can stall approvals.

8

Texas Governor Abbott calls for data centres to be banned in rural areas

Policy

Gov. Greg Abbott — historically one of the industry's strongest supporters — has called for data centres to be banned in rural Texas (July 3). It follows San Marcos becoming the first Texas city to ban data centres outright and a wave of local moratoriums across at least eight states.

Why it matters: When the governor of the fastest-growing data centre state turns against rural siting, the political ground under the Texas build-out is shifting — siting risk just rose across the ERCOT market.

QTS drops appeal, kills gigawatt PW Digital Gateway project in Virginia

PolicyConstruction

QTS has conceded defeat in its years-long battle to build the gigawatt-scale PW Digital Gateway campus in Prince William County, Virginia, dropping its appeal and ending the project (July 3).

Why it matters: A gigawatt of planned capacity dying in the world's largest data centre market shows community opposition can now kill even the biggest, best-funded projects — capacity forecasts for Northern Virginia need a haircut.
